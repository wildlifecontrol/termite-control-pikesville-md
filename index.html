What Is Termite Control?

Termite control entails identifying termite species, locating nests, and deciding on the most effective removal methods. In termite-infested areas, a combination of standard and advanced inspection techniques is critical.

Termite exterminators in Maryland recommend that homes with treated or resistant timber install both chemical and physical barriers to help prevent future infestations. Do not disturb the affected area if you find live termites or damaged timbers. It’s also not a good idea to use insecticides or sprays to get rid of termites. If termites are sufficiently disturbed, they will relocate and may not be discovered again until more obvious damage has been done.

Exterminators can use live termites to find their nests and eradicate the colony using various techniques such as dust or termite bait. Only an expert’s skill and judgment can be trusted to locate destructive termite species and signs of their presence. Based on professional training and extensive fieldwork in termite control in various situations, their judgment is considered the most reliable​

How We Remove Termite Infestations

There are several termite treatment options that do not require you to spend the night somewhere else. Heat treatment and green treatments are two of these options. Heat treatment is a termite control method that employs heat to kill termites. A professional will arrive to your home with specially constructed heaters and progressively raise the heat until termites are killed. This procedure can normally be completed in a single day. Because items in your home may be sensitive to heat damage, some preparation is essential. During the treatment, you may need to remove or switch off some items. There are several environmentally friendly termite treatments available, including orange oil, borates, and tree bark derivatives. Green termite treatment options can be used in your home while you and your family are present. They don't represent a threat to your family, but they can help you get rid of termites. In most cases, green treatment options can be applied in a single day.

When it comes to getting rid of termites in your home, you have several options. What you can expect from treatment depends on the type of treatment you require. The most time-consuming treatment option is fumigation. Only when structural fumigation is genuinely the best option is it recommended. Heat and green treatments will not have a significant impact on your daily life if you stay at home. If one of these options proves to be the most effective, termites can be eradicated in as little as one day. The most essential thing to know as you prepare for termite treatment is that once the treatment is completed, your home will be termite-free. Getting those pesky bugs out of your home is well worth the time and work it takes to prepare for treatment.

Home Inspection

Termite removal experts will spend about an hour and a half to two hours thoroughly inspecting your property during your termite inspection. The inspector will examine your home’s interior and exterior areas for visible signs of a termite infestation, such as droppings, broken wings, mud tubes, and damaged wood.

Termite Removal

Termite bait stations will be strategically placed in the soil around the home by experienced termite control professionals. The bait is spread to foraging termites after the system is installed. Termites will then share the bait until the entire colony, including the queen, has been eliminated, leaving no harmful chemicals behind.

Termite Clean-up & Damage Repair

Proper clean-up and termite damage repair are vital because they will prevent future infestations from occurring. An insect control technician will completely remove and harmful materials and identify any wood that needs to be repaired or replaced.
